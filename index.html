About Tawseel Foundation

Tawseel means both "delivery" and "connection" in Arabic, perfectly capturing our mission of delivering meaningful opportunities while building lasting connections within communities. We believe that sustainable change happens when people are supported through integrated pathways that address both immediate needs and long-term empowerment.

Our approach is unique because we don't just provide temporary assistance - we create a comprehensive ecosystem where each stage builds upon the previous one. From supporting a child's education to helping their parents develop new skills, to eventually providing microfinance for family businesses, we follow entire families and communities through their journey to independence.

Founded on the principle that every person deserves not just aid, but genuine pathways to self-sufficiency, Tawseel Foundation delivers hope, opportunity, and lasting transformation.

Stories of Transformation

Fatima's Complete Journey: From Student to Business Owner

Fatima entered our Foundation Stage at age 9, struggling academically and facing food insecurity at home. Through consistent tutoring, meals, and family support, she not only caught up but became a peer mentor by age 14. In our Development Stage, she learned digital marketing while teaching younger students. Today, at 24, Fatima runs a successful online tutoring platform through our Launch Stage microfinance program, employing five other program graduates and serving over 100 students across three communities. Her younger brother is now in our Development Stage, and her mother completed our adult literacy program.

The Al-Rashid Family: Breaking Generational Cycles

When we first connected with the Al-Rashid family, unemployment and limited education options seemed insurmountable. We began by supporting their youngest daughter, Zahra, through Foundation Stage schooling. As trust grew, we enrolled her father in our vocational training program and her mother in our women's empowerment initiative. Three years later, the family operates a small catering business, Zahra excels as a student leader, and they serve as community ambassadors, helping other families navigate our integrated programs. The transformation rippled through their entire neighborhood.

Community Impact: The Riverside Neighborhood

What started with supporting 12 children in the Riverside neighborhood has grown into a thriving community transformation. Today, 45 families are connected to our programs, 8 small businesses operate in the area, and a community learning center - started by our program graduates - serves as a hub for education and economic activity. The neighborhood now has its own parent-teacher association, youth leadership council, and micro-business cooperative, all led by Tawseel alumni who continue giving back.
